Donating through bone marrow

This is unlikely, but it's sometimes better for the patient

1. Have some tests

As with donating by blood, You go to a specialised hospital for some simple tests, we'll call to say the tests are fine, and then send you details about things like dates, hotels and transportation.

2. Go to the hospital

You travel with your companion to the hospital. You stay in the hospital and your companion stays in a hotel. Don't worry about the cost - we'll pay for it all.

3. The big day

In the morning, you'll be given a general anaesthetic. During a short procedure - about 30 minutes - you'll lie on your front and bone marrow is taken from your pelvic bone using a needle and syringe. You won't need stitches after the procedure but you'll have two plasters where the bone marrow was taken from. People think donating bone marrow is really painful, but donors tell us that it's not any worse than how you'd feel after a heavy workout in the gym

4. Stay overnight

You wake up and you might feel a bit bruised where the needles were inserted. Your companion arrives and you spend the day and one more night in hospital recovering from the anaesthetic before going home the next day.

5. Get plenty of rest

For the next few days you'll probably feel some pain where the needles were inserted, as well as general tiredness. Take some paracetamol and get some well-deserved rest. You'll soon be right as rain.

6. The difference it makes

The stem cells you've donated will be used to give someone with blood cancer that most special gift - the chance of life. Well done! That makes you pretty fantastic.
