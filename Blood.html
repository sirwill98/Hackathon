Blood Donations

Donating through blood

Nearly 90% of all of our donations are done this way

1. Have some tests

You go to a specialised hospital and have some tests done - blood tests, a chest x-ray, that sort of thing. A week later, we'll call to say the tests are fine, and then send you details about things like dates, hotels and transportation.

2. Daily injections

You'll receive a four day course of injections that stimulate your body to produce stem cells. A nurse comes to your office or home to give you the injections. You'll probably notice your bones aching a little. Don't worry, its totally normal.

3. Go to the hospital

You and your companion will then head to the hotel where you'll stay the night - don't worry, we pay for it all. The next morning, you'll head over to the hospital.

4. The big day

Now you're ready to donate. You're connected to a machine which takes blood from one arm, filters out the stem cells, and returns your blood to the other arm. The process takes four to five hours. It can get a bit boring, so you might want to watch telly or chat to your companion. If there are enough stem cells - and there usually are - you can go home. If not we need you to have another injection and return the next day for another session on the machine.

5. Get some rest

After your big day you'll probably feel tired and might experience some flu-like symptoms. These always pass after a few days. Get plenty of well-deserved rest and you'll soon be feeling right as rain

6. The difference it makes

Your stem cells are the things which are missing from the person you're a match for. Yours will kick-start there system into action and help them recover from a very vulnerable state. You've potentially saved a life. Amazing, huh?
