When you join the Anthony Nolan stem cell register, there are a few key facts we want you to confirm you’ve read, understood and agree with:

  • If you’re found to be a match for a patient in need, you understand that:
    • You will need to give some blood samples so we can confirm the match.
    • You could be asked to donate in one of two ways:
      • 90% of people donate stem cells via their bloodstream.
      • 10% of people donate via their bone marrow.
    • You might be asked to donate to a patient who lives anywhere in the world, but the donation will always take place in the UK.
    • The donation process will be completely anonymous to protect you and the patient.
  • You understand that you’ll stay on the register until you’re 60, unless you ask for your details to be removed.
  • You confirm that you’re not on the British Bone Marrow Registry, Welsh Bone Marrow Registry, DKMS UK or any other stem cell registers worldwide. (You only need to join one register!)
I confirm that I have read, understood and agree with the statements above.
